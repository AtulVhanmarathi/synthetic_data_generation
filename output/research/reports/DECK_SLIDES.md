## SLIDE 6 — INTELLIGENT OPERATIONS

**Headline:** Your IOC is running at human speed. It doesn't have to.

**Left panel — The current model:**
- 24/7 team manually coordinates: crew assignment, trip scheduling,
  weather rerouting, Jetfly partner flights, catering, slot reservations
- Every new program (CobaltPass, Sourcing Solution, Jetfly) adds a new
  category of coordination to the same human workflow
- Scale = headcount. That's a ceiling.

**Right panel — The intelligent model:**
- AI agent layer handles routine decision trees (crew eligibility, aircraft
  availability, weather thresholds) — humans handle judgment calls
- Predictive rerouting flags weather issues before clients call
- Atlas maintenance windows auto-factored into scheduling
- Jetfly and CaptainJet availability queried in real time

**Bottom — The question that opens this conversation:**
> *"What percentage of IOC decisions follow a repeatable rule — and what
> percentage genuinely require human judgment?"*

**Calfus connection (subtle, bottom right):**
Calfus builds AI agents for complex 24/7 operations. AssistIQ was built
for Oracle support environments with identical coordination patterns.

**Headline:** The world's only Level D PC-12 NGX simulator —

**Context:**
In February 2025, PlaneSense launched the world's first Pilatus PC-12 NGX

**The opportunity:**
